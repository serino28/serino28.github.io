Publications

  • Safe-Unsafe Concept Separation Emerges from a Single Direction in Language Models Activation Space ↗
    EACL 2026 — Rabat, Morocco

    We identify the layer where safe/unsafe concepts are maximally separable in pretrained LLMs. A simple linear classifier on internal representations provides robust safeguarding without weight modification, validated across 8 domains, 3 tasks, and 16 non-English languages.

    2026
  • SFAL: Semantic-Functional Alignment Scores for Distributional Evaluation of Auto-Interpretability in Sparse Autoencoders ↗
    EMNLP 2025 (Industry Track) — Suzhou, China

    We propose SFAL, a novel evaluation framework that measures how well LLM-generated feature explanations align with actual feature behavior. Unlike costly simulation-based methods, SFAL uses embedding-based semantic similarity for fast, scalable assessment of auto-interpretability quality.

    2025
  • Disce aut Deficere: Evaluating LLMs Proficiency on the INVALSI Italian Benchmark ↗
    ECML-PKDD 2025 — Porto, Portugal

    We introduce a rigorous benchmark using official Italian INVALSI standardized tests to evaluate LLMs' linguistic versatility beyond English. Our analysis reveals significant performance gaps between multilingual claims and actual non-English language understanding.

    2025
  • Towards the Terminator Economy: Assessing Job Exposure to AI through LLMs ↗
    IJCAI 2025 — Montreal, Canada

    We formalize TEAI (Task Exposure to AI Index), a data-driven framework using LLMs to systematically assess AI automation potential across U.S. occupations. Our methodology enables granular task-level analysis of workforce vulnerability to AI displacement.

    2025
  • SkiLLMo: Normalized ESCO Skill Extraction through Transformer Models ↗
    ACM SAC 2025 — Catania, Italy

    A complete pipeline combining Transformers and LLMs to extract skills from job advertisements and map them to the standardized ESCO taxonomy. Our approach addresses the critical challenge of skill normalization for labour market analytics.

    2025
  • An approach to Evaluative AI through Large Language Models ↗
    ECAI 2024 — Santiago de Compostela, Spain

    We introduce EADS (Evaluative AI Decision Support), a framework that integrates LLMs into decision-making processes by generating structured pros/cons evaluations. This enables transparent, conversational explanations that augment human judgment.

    2024
  • Augmenting XAI with LLMs: A Case Study in Banking Marketing Recommendation ↗
    XAI World Conference @ IJCAI 2024 — Malta 🏆

    We demonstrate how Generative AI can transform opaque ML model outputs into human-readable natural language explanations for recommender systems in banking. Awarded Best Presentation for bridging the gap between technical XAI and end-user understanding.

    2024
  • Skills-Hunter: adapting Large Language Models to the Labour Market for Skill Extraction ↗
    AIxIA 2023 — Rome, Italy

    Our pioneering work on leveraging LLMs for automated skill extraction from job postings, addressing the fundamental data labelling bottleneck that had limited previous approaches to labour market analysis.

    2023
